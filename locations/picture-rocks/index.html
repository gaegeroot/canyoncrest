Picture Rocks · Northwest Tucson, AZ

Where the desert
is the design.

Picture Rocks sits at the western edge of the Tucson metro where saguaro forest, creosote flats, and rocky terrain converge. Properties here are typically larger, less manicured, and surrounded by open desert - and the best landscapes we've done in this area lean fully into that character rather than trying to soften it.

Canyon Crest brings a light touch to Picture Rocks projects: preserving native growth, working with the natural drainage patterns, and using local rock rather than imported material wherever possible.

  • Native Sonoran plant preservation & supplemental planting
  • Natural boulder placement & dry-stack stonework
  • Wash-friendly drainage & erosion control
  • Minimal-intervention xeriscape design
  • Fire-safe perimeter clearing & replanting

Designing for this terrain

What makes Picture Rocks different.

Picture Rocks properties often exist at the edge of real wilderness. That means wash crossings, protected native plants, and terrain that hasn't been graded smooth. Our work out here starts with a close read of what's already there - a saguaro stand worth keeping, a natural wash that becomes a design feature, a boulder field that anchors the whole yard.

Wash Preservation

Natural washes crossing or adjacent to the property are respected and designed around - they're a feature, not a problem to fix.

Native Retention

We inventory existing Saguaro, Palo Verde, and Ironwood before any work begins and build the design around what's worth keeping.

Local Stone First

Sonoran fieldstone and on-site boulders are used as the primary hardscape material - imported manufactured stone is avoided.

Wildfire Interface

Properties at the urban-wildland interface get thoughtful fuel-management planning without stripping the desert character that makes the area special.
